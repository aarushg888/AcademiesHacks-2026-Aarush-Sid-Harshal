Ray Dalio on principles-based thinking: "I believe that having principles is the most important thing for success. Without principles, you'd be forced to react to circumstances and you'd be at the mercy of those circumstances. Principles help you be consistent in your approach and to learn from mistakes."

Ray Dalio on the debt cycle: "Understanding the debt cycle is essential. There are short-term debt cycles of 5–8 years and long-term debt cycles of 75–100 years. Most people only see the short cycle. You must understand where we are in the long-term debt cycle to understand major investment risks."

Ray Dalio on diversification through correlation: "The holy grail of investing is to find 15 or more good, uncorrelated return streams. A portfolio of uncorrelated assets with similar returns will reduce risk by 80% compared to any individual asset. Most people are far less diversified than they think."

Ray Dalio on the economic machine: "The economy works like a machine — driven by productivity growth, short-term debt cycles, and long-term debt cycles. When you understand the mechanics, market movements become understandable rather than mysterious."

Ray Dalio on risk parity: "Most portfolios are dominated by equity risk — even a 60/40 portfolio is 90% driven by equities in risk terms. True balance means balancing risk contributions, not dollar allocations. Every asset class has its environment where it performs best."

Ray Dalio on radical transparency: "The greatest tragedy of mankind is that people have politeness and modesty suppress critical information. In my portfolio and my company, I want radical transparency — all mistakes must be examined openly so we can learn from them."

Ray Dalio on macro environments: "There are four environments: rising growth, falling growth, rising inflation, falling inflation. Every asset class has an environment it likes. Gold does well in inflation. Bonds do well in deflation and falling growth. Equities do well in rising growth and low inflation."

Ray Dalio on being wrong: "I learned that there is an incredible beauty to mistakes because embedded in each mistake is a puzzle — how could I have done it better? I embrace being wrong and failing if I can learn from it quickly."

Ray Dalio on currencies and reserves: "No reserve currency lasts forever. The dollar's reserve status has benefited America enormously but it is not permanent. Watch the long-term trends in debt, currency debasement, and global power shifts — they move slowly until they move fast."

Ray Dalio on gold: "I believe that gold should be a significant portion of every portfolio. Central banks cannot print gold. In a world where governments are printing enormous amounts of money, gold is the hedge against monetary debasement."

Ray Dalio on stress-testing: "You have to stress-test your portfolio for every environment including the ones that seem impossible. What happens if inflation hits 15%? What happens if the dollar loses reserve status? Prepare for the tail risks even when they seem far-fetched."

Ray Dalio on geopolitics: "The rise of China and the relative decline of American power is one of the most important macro forces of our time. This changes trade flows, currency dynamics, and which markets will compound over the next 50 years."

Ray Dalio on human emotion in markets: "Markets move in ways that reflect human nature — they overshoot in both directions because humans are emotional. Understanding crowd psychology is as important as understanding fundamentals in predicting near-term price action."

Ray Dalio on systems thinking: "Build a system, not a feeling. If your investment process depends on how you feel on a given day, you will fail. The most successful investors I know have codified their thinking into rules and they follow those rules even when it's painful."
